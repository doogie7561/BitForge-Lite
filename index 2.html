Why Lightning Matters

Lightning can move many small payments without putting every individual payment directly into a Bitcoin block. That is the realistic path toward “more transactions than normal” while still settling back to Bitcoin.

Learn

Plain-English explanations built into the app.

What mining actually does

Mining collects valid transactions into a block and searches for a proof-of-work result that Bitcoin nodes will accept.

Why lightweight does not mean faster Bitcoin

A lightweight app can be efficient, but it cannot make Bitcoin nodes accept blocks that break Bitcoin consensus rules.

What batching does

Batching combines multiple payments into one transaction so repeated overhead is reduced.

What Lightning does

Lightning allows many fast small payments off-chain, then final balances can settle back onto Bitcoin.

What fees are

Fees are paid to miners. Higher fees usually make a transaction more attractive when blocks are crowded.

What v1 avoids

This shell avoids private keys, seed phrases, wallet signing, and real payments. Those should be handled carefully later.
